How to execute — via stock futures
In India you can't carry an equity short overnight in the cash segment (intraday only; SLB is illiquid), and a pairs trade holds for days. So both legs are stock futures:
  • Long leg → BUY futures, short leg → SELL futures (near-month, most liquid). Both held overnight until exit.
  • Whole lots, beta-neutral. Size so valueB ≈ |β|·valueA, rounded to lots. The minimum here is 1 lot each (~₹0.9–1.7M notional).
  • Margin ≈ 20% of notional per leg (SPAN+exposure). Two different underlyings usually get no cross-margin offset, so budget margin on both legs (unless your broker offers portfolio margin).
  • Rollover: stock futures expire the last Thursday monthly. If a trade is still open near expiry, roll both legs to next month (square off + re-enter) 1–2 days before — don't let it expire.
  • Exit: square off both futures when z reverts to target (~0.5); stop if z hits 3.5→4.5; time-stop at 2× half-life.
